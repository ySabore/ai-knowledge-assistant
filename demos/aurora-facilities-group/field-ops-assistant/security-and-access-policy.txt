Aurora Facilities Group
Security and Access Policy

Purpose
This policy defines minimum security practices for Aurora personnel who handle building access, temporary badges, escort procedures, and security-sensitive operational information.

Access control rules
- Employees may only use credentials assigned to them personally.
- Badge sharing is prohibited.
- Technicians must not tailgate through secured doors unless site security has approved escorted entry.
- Access to data centers, executive suites, pharmacy storage, records rooms, and alarm panels is limited to explicitly authorized personnel.

Visitor and vendor escorting
- Unbadged vendors and visitors must follow customer escort requirements.
- Escort responsibility remains with the Aurora employee until the visitor is handed off to an approved site contact.
- Visitors may not be left unattended in restricted zones.

Alarm and code handling
- Alarm codes, key safe combinations, and lockbox instructions must only be shared through approved channels.
- Codes may not be sent by personal text message.
- Written copies of alarm instructions must be stored in approved systems only.

Incident handling
- Suspicious behavior, forced entry, missing keys, or unauthorized access attempts must be escalated immediately.
- Security incidents should be documented with time, location, involved parties, and the immediate containment action.
- Aurora employees should not physically intervene unless there is an immediate life safety requirement and they are trained to do so.

Device and record security
- Site photos, badge logs, and access incident notes must remain in company-managed systems.
- Lost or stolen devices with customer information must be reported to IT within one hour.
- Customer floorplans and camera screenshots may only be shared with authorized customer contacts.

After-hours building entry
- After-hours entry requires active ticket documentation or supervisor approval.
- Technicians must verify that the visit purpose matches the dispatch notes before entering restricted spaces.
- If the listed site contact cannot be reached for a questionable after-hours request, the technician must pause entry and call the on-call manager.
