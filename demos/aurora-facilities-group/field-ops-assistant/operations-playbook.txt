Aurora Facilities Group
Field Operations Playbook
Revision: February 2026

Shift start checklist
1. Review assigned tickets, active incidents, and on-call notes.
2. Confirm site address, access instructions, parking details, and customer point of contact.
3. Verify tools, PPE, replacement supplies, and any required permits or escort notes.
4. Check whether the site has restricted zones, after-hours badge procedures, or loading dock timing rules.

Dispatch expectations
- Dispatch creates or updates every service ticket before a technician arrives on site.
- Field technicians must acknowledge dispatch contact before travel begins.
- If a technician cannot make the estimated arrival time, dispatch must be updated immediately so the customer can be notified.

On-site arrival protocol
- Sign in according to the customer building rules.
- Introduce yourself to the site contact or security desk.
- Confirm the exact issue location and whether there are occupant safety or access concerns.
- Take a quick condition snapshot before work begins when the issue involves leaks, damage, blocked access, or repeat failures.

Priority 1 response
- Stabilize the area first.
- Protect occupants and property before attempting a full repair.
- Escalate to the on-call manager if life safety, building evacuation, or major service interruption is involved.
- Create updates every 30 minutes until the issue is stabilized.

Badge and key handling
- Keys may never be left unattended in carts, vehicles, or unsecured offices.
- Temporary badges must be returned before leaving the site unless the site lead authorizes overnight hold procedures.
- Lost keys, badges, or access cards must be reported immediately to dispatch and security leadership.

Vendor coordination
- External vendors must sign in and be escorted where customer rules require it.
- Aurora remains responsible for customer communication even when a third-party vendor performs the repair.
- Vendor arrival delays longer than 30 minutes should be relayed to the customer contact.

Closure standards
- Close tickets with the action taken, materials used, remaining risk, and follow-up owner.
- If the issue is only partially resolved, document the temporary mitigation clearly.
- Photos should be attached for leaks, damage, blocked access, and visible repair completion when permitted by the customer.
