Aurora Facilities Group
Workplace Safety Basics
Audience: Corporate HR and site supervisors

General principles
- Safety is a condition of employment for all Aurora team members and subcontracted support staff working under Aurora direction.
- Employees must stop work and escalate when they encounter unsafe conditions that are not covered by the current task plan.
- Supervisors are responsible for reinforcing safety expectations during onboarding, shift huddles, and incident follow-up.

Required reporting
- Near misses, injuries, unsafe access points, electrical hazards, and chemical exposure concerns must be reported the same day.
- Any employee requiring medical attention beyond first aid must trigger an incident report before the end of shift when practical.
- HR must be notified of recordable incidents, restricted duty assignments, and workers' compensation claims.

Personal protective equipment
- Minimum PPE for field operations includes closed-toe shoes and task-appropriate gloves when handling cleaning agents, waste, tools, or rough materials.
- Eye protection is required when there is risk of splash, dust, or flying particles.
- High-visibility vests are required in loading dock zones, parking structures, and active traffic routes.
- Hearing protection is required when employees work near loud mechanical equipment for extended periods.

Slip, trip, and fall prevention
- Wet floor signage must be placed before mopping or spill remediation begins.
- Extension cords may not cross egress routes unless protected and approved for temporary use.
- Storage rooms, stairwells, and electrical rooms must remain clear of obstructions.
- Damaged flooring, loose stair treads, or poor lighting must be reported to site management immediately.

Chemical safety
- Employees may only use approved chemicals listed in the site supply catalog.
- Secondary containers must be labeled.
- Safety Data Sheets must be accessible on site or through the approved digital system.
- Mixing bleach with ammonia or other unapproved substances is prohibited.

Emergency response
- Employees must know the building-specific evacuation assembly area for each assigned site.
- Security and site leadership should be contacted immediately for fire alarms, suspicious packages, forced entry, or medical emergencies.
- During severe weather, supervisors may reassign staff to building lockdown, occupant communication, or damage assessment duties.

Return-to-work
- HR coordinates return-to-work planning with the employee, site supervisor, and insurance carrier when applicable.
- Modified duty assignments should be documented with start date, physical restrictions, and review date.

Training cadence
- Safety orientation is required for all new hires before solo site assignment.
- Refresher safety training is required annually.
- Additional task-specific training is required for ladder work, lockout-tagout support, chemical handling, and powered equipment use.
