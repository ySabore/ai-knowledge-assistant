Aurora Facilities Group
Employee Handbook Summary
Version: January 2026

Company overview
Aurora Facilities Group provides facilities support, janitorial coordination, access control support, and site operations services for commercial buildings, healthcare campuses, and light industrial properties across the Mid-Atlantic region.

Employment classifications
- Full-time employees are scheduled for 30 hours or more per week.
- Part-time employees are scheduled for fewer than 30 hours per week.
- Field technicians may be assigned rotating schedules, evening shifts, and weekend coverage depending on customer contracts.
- Temporary labor may be used for project work, seasonal demand, or emergency response assignments.

Attendance expectations
- Employees must confirm availability and assigned location before the start of every shift.
- Team members who will be late by more than 10 minutes must notify both their direct supervisor and the site lead.
- No-call/no-show incidents are escalated to HR after one occurrence and may result in corrective action.
- Repeated attendance issues may affect eligibility for preferred shifts, overtime assignments, and annual performance reviews.

Timekeeping
- All employees must clock in and out through the approved scheduling or payroll system.
- Time records must reflect the actual worksite and shift worked.
- Meal breaks longer than 30 minutes must be recorded.
- Supervisors may not edit time records without employee acknowledgment unless required to correct a payroll error.

Paid time off
- PTO requests should be submitted at least 10 calendar days in advance when possible.
- Blackout periods may apply during weather events, major customer transitions, and year-end building coverage.
- Managers approve PTO based on staffing coverage, contract obligations, and seniority.
- Sick leave should be reported as soon as the employee determines they are unable to report to work.

Conduct standards
- Employees must treat customers, occupants, visitors, and coworkers respectfully.
- Harassment, retaliation, discrimination, threats, and intimidation are prohibited.
- Property access badges, keys, alarm codes, and camera credentials may only be used for assigned work.
- Posting customer data, security footage, floorplans, or incident details to personal devices or social media is prohibited.

Uniform and appearance
- Field staff must wear approved uniforms, closed-toe shoes, and any required high-visibility or protective equipment.
- ID badges must remain visible when working on a client site unless the customer requires alternate credential placement.
- Damaged uniforms or missing PPE must be reported to the supervisor before the shift begins.

Devices and confidentiality
- Company-issued phones, tablets, and laptops are for business use.
- Customer building access procedures, maintenance logs, visitor records, and incident reports are confidential.
- Personal USB storage devices may not be connected to company equipment.
- Lost devices must be reported to IT and the employee's manager within one hour of discovery.

Corrective action
- Aurora generally uses coaching, written warning, final warning, and termination as the standard path.
- Serious misconduct, including theft, violence, deliberate badge misuse, or falsified records, may result in immediate termination.

Questions about policy
Employees should first ask their supervisor, then HR, if policy language conflicts with local site instructions.
