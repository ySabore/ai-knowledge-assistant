Aurora Facilities Group
Purchase Order Rules

Scope
These rules apply to Aurora employees who request supplies, tools, subcontractor labor, uniform replacements, and emergency materials through the procurement process.

Approval thresholds
- Up to $500: site lead or department manager approval
- $501 to $2,500: department manager plus procurement approval
- $2,501 to $10,000: procurement manager plus finance approval
- Above $10,000: finance director and operations vice president approval

Emergency purchases
- Emergency purchases are allowed only when delaying purchase would create a safety issue, major customer disruption, or significant property damage risk.
- The requester must document why standard approval timing could not be followed.
- Emergency purchases still require next-business-day review by procurement.

Required PO details
- business justification
- customer site or cost center
- vendor name
- itemized quantity and pricing
- delivery location
- requested delivery date
- requester name and approver

Restricted purchasing
- Personal purchases may never be placed on Aurora accounts.
- Gift cards, cash equivalents, and non-approved software subscriptions require finance preapproval regardless of amount.
- Purchases from new vendors may not proceed until procurement confirms onboarding requirements are complete.

Receiving and reconciliation
- The receiving employee must verify quantity and visible condition within one business day of delivery.
- Missing, damaged, or substituted items must be reported to procurement immediately.
- Invoices must match the approved purchase order before payment release.

Subcontractor labor
- Scope of work, schedule, insurance status, and site access requirements must be documented before the subcontractor arrives on site.
- Any field-requested change order must be approved before the subcontractor performs out-of-scope work unless emergency conditions apply.
